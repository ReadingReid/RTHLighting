Lighting Designer and Electrician

Long Lake Camp for the Arts has a total of three theater spaces. To the left is the rep plot for the Rylee theater, the biggest of the three. This space was mainly used for the largest musicals with the oldest casts, and was also the host to Mintons, the penultimate performing art night. I designed three shows in this space, including Chicago, 13, and a Hansel and Gretel themed dance, as well as maintained the space as an electrician.

Located below are the rep plots for the other two theaters, the Performing Arts Center, or PAC, and the Caine's theater. The PAC was used for musicals as well as weekly rock nights and performing arts nights. I designed The Drowsy Chaperone, The Little Mermaid, and Peter and the Starcatcher twice in this space, while also rotating out with the other lighting designers in running the rock and performing arts nights. The Caine's theater was the host of all the straight plays for the summer. I maintained both the PAC and Caine's theater as an electrician as well.
